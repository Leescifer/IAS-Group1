Relationships among laws and regulations on information system

Angeline

Functions of Laws on Information Security: Laws establish the foundational legal frameworks that mandate the protection of information. They define rights, responsibilities, and penalties related to information security. Key functions include:

  • Setting Standards: Laws outline what constitutes secure handling of information.
  • Protecting Rights: They ensure individuals' rights to privacy and data protection are upheld.
  • Enforcing Accountability: Violations, such as data breaches or unauthorized access, result in legal penalties, creating a deterrent against negligence or malicious actions.

For example, in the Philippines, the Data Privacy Act of 2012 mandates organizations to protect personal information and ensure its secure handling.

Angilo

2. Functions of Regulations on Information Security: Regulations operationalize laws by providing specific, actionable guidelines for compliance. They translate legal principles into concrete measures for organizations to implement. Key functions include: Detailing Compliance Requirements: Regulations specify how organizations should secure data (e.g., encryption, access controls). Facilitating Enforcement: They guide regulatory agencies in monitoring and penalizing non-compliance. Encouraging Consistency: By standardizing security practices, regulations ensure uniformity across industries. For example, the Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR) of the Data Privacy Act in the Philippines offer detailed guidelines on processing personal data and reporting breaches.

Charlene

3. Relationships Between Laws and Regulations: Laws and regulations work in synergy to create a robust information security framework: Interdependence: Laws establish the broad legal requirements, while regulations provide the operational details for implementation. Complementarity: Laws define the "what" (e.g., data must be protected), and regulations define the "how" (e.g., use of encryption or access controls). Enforcement Mechanism: Regulatory bodies use regulations to monitor compliance with laws, investigate violations, and impose penalties. Together, this synergy ensures that organizations not only understand their obligations but also have clear guidelines to protect sensitive information effectively.

Summary of Cybersecurity and Data Privacy Laws Philippine Data Privacy Act of 2012 Aims to protect individuals' privacy while allowing the free flow of information. It penalizes unauthorized processing of personal data. Cybersecurity Information Sharing Act (CISA) Enables businesses to monitor and secure their systems and, with consent, assist others. It safeguards businesses sharing cybersecurity threat data with the government. Children’s Online Privacy Protection Rule (COPPA) Regulates the online collection of data from children under 13, setting strict limits on data gathering by companies.
